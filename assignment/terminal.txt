# 8 - Why would you run the command 'git status'?
'git status' will show in red, a list of files that have been changed but not staged 
to be added for your next commit.  
If the files have been staged for your next commit they will be listed in green.
# 9 - What does the command 'git add .' do?
'git add .' will stage all files that are being tracked by your git repo in your current working directory.
Staged meaning that they will be included in your next commit.
